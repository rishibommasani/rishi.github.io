Rishi Bommasani

Hi! I'm a first-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Stanford University, affiliated with the Stanford NLP group and the Stanford AI Laboratory.
In the summer and fall I will be working with Percy Liang and in the winter I will be working with both Dan Jurafsky and Tatsu Hashimoto.

My graduate studies are funded by a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. [Press Release]

I graduated with a M.S. in Computer Science in 2020, B.A. in Computer Science in 2019, and a B.A. in Mathematics in 2019, all from Cornell University.
Throughout my time there, I was exceedingly fortunate to be advised by the amazing Claire Cardie.
In many ways, during my four years at Cornell, Cornell CS was my home. The department wrote this nice piece about my time at Cornell. [Profile] [Profile 2]

My research focuses on building and understanding NLP systems in a scientifically principled and theoretically sound manner.
Research Interests: Interpretability, Social Bias and Fairness, Information Theory, Algorithms, Privacy, Psycholinguistics, and Cognitive Science.
