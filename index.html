Rishi Bommasani

Hi! I'm a second-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at Stanford University, affiliated with the Stanford NLP group and the Stanford AI Laboratory.
I am blessed to be advised by Percy Liang and Dan Jurafsky.
My graduate studies are currently funded by the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship Program (NSF GRFP). [Press Release]

I graduated with a bachelors (Mathematics, Computer Science; 2019) and masters (Computer Science; 2020) from Cornell University.
Throughout my time there, I was exceedingly fortunate to be advised by the incomparable Claire Cardie.
I was very fortunate to learn from and collaborate with the late Professor Arzoo Katiyar, who I profoundly miss and am deeply grateful to.
During my four years at Cornell, Cornell CS was my home. The department wrote this nice piece about my time at Cornell. [Profile] [Profile 2]

My research focuses on understanding AI systems and their social impact, as well as using NLP to further scientific inquiry.
Primary Research Interests: Foundation Models, Measurement and Evaluation, Model Analysis and Interpretability, Social Impact of AI
Secondary Research Interests: Computational Social Science, Psycholinguistics and Cognitive Science, Algorithms and TCS, Privacy
